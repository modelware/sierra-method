The requirements in this document are organized by hierarchical categories that reflect the level of abstraction and traceability within the system engineering process. Each requirement is assigned to a specific level, such as **Level 1 (L1)**, **Level 2 (L2)**, and so on, to indicate its position in the mission architecture and its degree of decomposition.

- **Level 1 (L1) – Mission-Level Requirements**  
    These requirements define the overarching goals and objectives of the mission. They are typically driven by stakeholder needs and strategic intent, and they establish the foundational success criteria for the entire mission.
    
- **Level 2 (L2) – Project-Level Requirements**  
    Derived from the mission-level requirements, these requirements specify what the project must deliver in order to fulfill the mission objectives. They define the major capabilities, constraints, and interfaces to be addressed by the project team.
    
- **Level 3 (L3) – System-Level Requirements**  
    These are detailed requirements that describe the behavior, performance, and design constraints of the system-of-interest. They guide subsystem decomposition, interface definition, and component-level development.
    
- Additional levels (e.g., **L4**, **L5**) may be used as necessary to capture further decomposition and refinement of requirements into subsystems, components, and implementation details.

This structured organization supports clear traceability, facilitates requirement allocation and validation, and aligns with systems engineering best practices. Each requirement is labeled accordingly and grouped under its respective category throughout the document.
